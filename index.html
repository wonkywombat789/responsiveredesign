redesigning nami restaurant's website

background information

A restaurant's website allows it the opportunity to share information with the viewer and attract customers. If the webpage is not designed well, viewers may miss key information that could draw them to your restaurant.

I decided to redesign a local restaurant's webpage to increase its efficiency, learnability, and memorability.

In order to redesign the page I first identified problems with the site, then I designed a low-fi wireframe to improve the interface. After creating the low-fi wireframe I then made a Hi-fi prototype on Figma. With the design curated, I was able to build the page using HTML/CSS

the problems

Before getting into my redesign, I first determined areas where the original website could be improved in terms of usability and design. I categorized the issues in terms of efficiency, learnability and memorability.

efficiency

  • really hard to read the text on the page, so viewers have to spend more time deciphering the information
  • some of the menu bar buttons don't lead anywhere, so users may get confused and spend more time trying to navigate to different parts of the page
  • could not find the hours and location of the restaurant easily, which is important information for customers

learnability

  • confusing and cluttered layout with a bunch of text on the home page, so it is hard to know what the information is
  • when the page is shrunk, the menu bar covers with information on the page making it harder to read
  • images on the home page make it hard to decipher what food the restaurant serves

memorability

  • text is hard to read which makes the information not memorable

accessibility

As mentioned above, the main issue with the website is the color scheme which makes it really hard to read the text on the page. When I used the web accessibility evaluation tool, 36 contrast errors were found.

As a non visually impaired person, I already had a very hard time deciphering the text on this page. This page is inaccessible to colorblind individuals as there is practically no contrast.

low-fi wireframe

After identifying issues in the orignal page, I started my redesign. To do this, I created low-fi wireframes on Balsamiq for three different screen sized (phone, tablet, and desktop). Doing a low-fi prototype for three different screen sizes allowed me to think carefully about the different layout choices for different screen sizes. I knew that things would have to be more vertically oriented for a smaller screen size to avoid horizontal squishing.

visual design stytle guide

After finishing my low-fi wireframes, I created a visual design guide on figma to ensure my hi-fi prototype was consistent. I designed to use dark green as the main color with light pink accent as those two colors have high contrast. I was able to input this color scheme on a colorblind checker and they seemed to be accessible.
